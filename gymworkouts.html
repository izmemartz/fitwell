Gym Workouts for Beginners

Learn basic exercises for each muscle group using a simple PPL (Push–Pull–Legs) structure.

What is PPL?

PPL stands for Push, Pull, Legs. It's a simple gym workout structure that divides exercises based on the movement patterns: Push day focuses on chest, shoulders, and triceps, Pull day targets back, biceps, and rear delts, and Legs day works your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. This structure helps beginners balance training, recover properly, and hit all major muscle groups effectively.

Push Day (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

Chest – Dumbbell Bench Press

Guide: Lie flat on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand. Push the weights up until arms are fully extended, then slowly lower them back to chest level. Keep your back pressed against the bench and move in a controlled manner. Aim for 3 sets of 8–12 reps.

Shoulders – Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Guide: Sit or stand with dumbbells at shoulder height. Press the weights overhead until arms are fully extended, then slowly lower them back down. Keep your core tight to avoid leaning back. Perform 3 sets of 8–12 reps.

Triceps – Tricep Pushdowns

Guide: Using a cable machine, grab the handle with an overhand grip. Keep elbows tucked by your sides and push the handle down until arms are fully extended. Slowly return to starting position. Aim for 3 sets of 10–15 reps.

Pull Day (Back, Biceps, Rear Delts)

Back – Lat Pulldown

Guide: Sit at a lat pulldown machine and grip the bar wider than shoulder-width. Pull the bar down toward your chest while squeezing your shoulder blades together, then slowly return to starting position. Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps.

Biceps – Dumbbell Bicep Curls

Guide: Stand with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward. Curl the weights toward your shoulders while keeping elbows close to your torso. Slowly lower back down. Perform 3 sets of 10–15 reps.

Rear Delts – Bent-Over Dumbbell Lateral Raises

Guide: Hold a dumbbell in each hand and bend slightly forward at your hips, keeping a straight back. With a slight bend in your elbows, lift the dumbbells out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the floor, focusing on squeezing your rear delts. Slowly lower the weights back down. Perform 3 sets of 12–15 reps.

Leg Day (Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes)

Quads – Leg Press

Guide: Sit on the leg press machine with feet shoulder-width on the platform. Push the platform away by extending your legs, then slowly return to starting position. Keep your back flat against the seat. 3 sets of 10–12 reps.

Hamstrings – Seated Leg Curl

Guide: Sit on the leg curl machine and position your legs under the pad. Curl your legs down as far as possible, then slowly return. Focus on controlled movements. 3 sets of 12–15 reps.

Glutes – Glute Bridges

Guide: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Lift your hips toward the ceiling by squeezing your glutes, then slowly lower back down. Keep your core engaged. 3 sets of 12–15 reps.
