Keto Guide

Learn how to achieve ketosis effectively with low-carb, high-fat meals and consistent keto-friendly nutrition.

What is Keto?

The ketogenic diet is a low-carb, high-fat diet that puts your body into a metabolic state called ketosis. In ketosis, your body burns fat for energy instead of carbohydrates. This helps with fat loss, energy stability, and supports overall health.

Keto Nutrition Basics

Low Carb, High Fat

Limit carbohydrates to 20–50g per day. Focus on healthy fats like olive oil, avocado, nuts, and fatty fish to meet your energy needs while maintaining ketosis.

Protein in Moderation

Protein intake should be moderate, typically 1.2–1.7g per kg of bodyweight. Too much protein can interfere with ketosis. Include eggs, meat, poultry, and dairy in your meals.

Proper Keto Meal Recipe

PESTO KETO CAULIFLOWER PIZZA

A keto-friendly pizza made with a crispy cauliflower crust, topped with pesto and nutrient-dense ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes, raw: 73g
  • Olive oil: 52g
  • Avocado, raw: 87g
  • Onions, raw: 30g
  • Mushrooms, raw, portabella: 37g
  • Olives, green: 38g
  • Egg, hard-boiled: 120g
  • Butter: 10g
  • Cucumber, raw: 64g
  • Pork chop, lean and fat: 200g
  • Yogurt, plain, whole milk: 245g
  • Cheese, parmesan: 70g
  • Lemon juice, raw: 15g
  • Spinach, raw: 25g
  • Pumpkin seeds, unsalted: 10g

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C), pulse cauliflower until rice-like, then sauté to remove moisture.
  2. Mix cauliflower with egg, cheese, salt, and pepper, press onto a lined tray, bake 20–25 min until firm.
  3. Spread pesto, add toppings like mozzarella, cherry tomatoes, spinach, or grilled chicken.
  4. Bake 8–10 more minutes until cheese melts.
  5. Cool slightly, slice, and serve warm.
