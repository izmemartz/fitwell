OMAD Guide

One Meal A Day: a simplified eating method focused on discipline, nutrient density, and metabolic efficiency.

What is OMAD?

OMAD stands for One Meal A Day, a fasting method where all calories are consumed within a single meal window. People choose OMAD to improve discipline, simplify nutrition, boost fat-burning, and maintain lean muscle while eating nutrient-rich meals.

Benefits of OMAD

Metabolic Improvement

OMAD helps improve insulin sensitivity and promotes fat loss while preserving muscle mass.

Time & Discipline

With one meal per day, meal planning becomes easier, and it helps build discipline and focus.

Simple OMAD Meal Plan (One Full Balanced Meal)

Main Plate

• Lean protein (chicken breast, beef, or tofu)
• 1-2 cups vegetables
• 1 cup rice, pasta, or potatoes

Side Options

• Salad with olive oil dressing
• Soup (chicken, vegetable, or miso)
• Fruit cup

Healthy Fats

• Avocado slices
• Nuts (almonds/cashews)
• Olive oil or peanut butter

After-Meal

• Greek yogurt
• Protein shake (optional)
• Dark chocolate (1–2 squares)

Proper OMAD Meal

CHICKEN BURRITO BOWL

Ingredients

Roasted chicken, olive oil, cilantro, sour cream, avocado, fresh lime juice, hot chili peppers, tomato, cucumber, cheddar cheese, paprika, dried oregano, ground cumin, sea salt, and black pepper.

Method

  1. Season chicken with olive oil, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and cumin. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes.
  2. Cook rice while preparing toppings: corn, black beans, shredded lettuce, salsa, chopped tomatoes, cheese, and avocado.
  3. Slice baked chicken into strips or cubes. Assemble bowl with rice, beans, corn, chicken, vegetables, salsa, and cheese.
  4. Optional: Bake the assembled bowl for 5–8 minutes to melt the cheese and warm everything together.

OMAD Tips

  • Stay hydrated throughout the day.
  • Choose nutrient-dense foods for your single meal.
  • Include protein and healthy fats to stay full.
  • Keep electrolytes balanced: sodium, potassium, magnesium.
  • Be consistent, your body adapts over time.
