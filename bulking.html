Bulking Guide

Gain muscle mass effectively through a calorie surplus, proper nutrition, and consistent strength training.

What is Bulking?

Bulking is a fitness phase focused on building muscle mass. This is done through a calorie surplus, high-protein intake, progressive strength training, and proper recovery. Bulking is about strategic nutrition and training to maximize muscle growth without gaining excessive fat.

Calorie Surplus Basics

The Smart Surplus

Aim for 250–500 extra calories above maintenance daily. This ensures muscle growth while minimizing fat gain. Focus on nutrient-dense foods that support training and recovery.

Protein Priority

Protein is crucial for muscle repair and growth. Target 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight. Include lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and plant-based proteins in every meal.

Simple Bulking Meal Plan (2500–2800 kcal)

Breakfast

• Oatmeal with milk and nuts
• 3 boiled eggs
• Smoothie with protein powder

Lunch

• Grilled chicken or beef
• 1–2 cups veggies
• 1 cup rice or pasta

Dinner

• Salmon or lean meat
• Mixed greens
• Sweet potato or quinoa

Snacks

• Greek yogurt
• Nuts (handful)
• Protein bar or shake

Muscle-Building Recipe

HIGH-PROTEIN CHICKEN BOWL

Ingredients

• 200g chicken breast
• 1 cup brown rice
• 1 cup steamed broccoli
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Salt, pepper, garlic powder to taste

Method

  1. Cook rice according to package instructions.
  2. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Grill or bake chicken until fully cooked.
  4. Steam broccoli until tender.
  5. Assemble rice, chicken, and broccoli in a bowl, drizzle olive oil.

Bulking Tips

  • Eat consistently and don’t skip meals.
  • Prioritize protein in every meal.
  • Train progressively heavier with proper form.
  • Get 7–9 hours of sleep for recovery.
  • Track calories and macros for steady gains.
